This project was designed to explore the Justinianic plague and the waves of plague that followed this initial outbreak in 541 AD. The sources, initial maps, and secondary content focus on the period between 541 and 750. The current goal, however, is to invite and encourage scholars and researchers in different disciplines to collaborate on a study of plagues before the Black Death by participating in translating, mapping, and analyzing data collected about these early epidemics. By creating an open space for crowdsourced scholarship, this project seeks to understand the spread of early disease and its impact on the development of ancient and early medieval worlds.
