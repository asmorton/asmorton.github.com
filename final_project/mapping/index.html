There are many ways to map historical events. The plagues for this project, for instance, can be studied by tracing the trade and missionary routes across the Mediterranean, as well as by looking at political influence and military movement. A combination of these maps, and any others you might want to contribute, can add to our understanding of how epidemics and plague language move across space and time.

Trade

Trade maps can be particularly useful, because they tell us how groups of people moved across land and sea, and how these routes interact with the major cities listed in our sources.

Empires

Understanding the growth and death of empires can improve our understanding of how people move, particularly in large groups, and why some areas that were once heavily populated can become barren.

Invasions

For our time period, invasions and military campaigns are very important. Procopius tracks the plague to the front lines of the war with the Persians, and Muslim soldiers encounter it in Syria.
